Casado’s Chance and Mallorca’s Threat as Barça Look to Stay Top

February 7, 2026
  • Hansi Flick hands Marc Casado a statement start ahead of Frenkie de Jong as Barça host Mallorca at a packed Camp Nou.
  • Leaders Barça eye a sixth straight win and the chance to move four points clear before Real Madrid visit Mestalla.
  • Mallorca arrive more dangerous than their league position suggests, led by in-form striker Vedat Muriqi and livewire forward Jan Virgili.
  • Fermín López signs a long-term extension to 2031 and openly dreams of wearing the captain’s armband.
  • Barça learn they’ll face Atlético Madrid in the Copa del Rey semi-finals, with key Atleti midfielder Pablo Barrios ruled out of the first leg.
  • Barça Femení keep dominating Clásicos while injury to defender Laia Aleixandri casts a shadow over an otherwise buoyant day.

Matchday at Camp Nou arrives with a different kind of tension. Barcelona sit atop La Liga, on a five-game winning streak in all competitions, and yet Hansi Flick’s team sheet for the visit of Mallorca immediately tells its own story. The coach has backed training-ground form and physical data over reputation, starting holding midfielder Marc Casado ahead of star playmaker Frenkie de Jong, who is on the bench after what Flick described as not being “100%.”

Casado’s inclusion is more than a one-off rotation. It signals how ruthlessly meritocratic this Barça has become. Flick is leaning into the energy, pressing and positional discipline that the academy midfielder offers at the base of midfield, trusting him to screen a back four built around the composure of young centre-back Pau Cubarsí. In front of them, the creative load is spread between the late-arriving runs of Fermín López and the line-breaking passing of Dani Olmo.

Up top, the front line is as aggressive as it is glamorous. On the right, Lamine Yamal comes into the game as arguably the form winger in Europe, scoring in four straight matches and looking every bit the superstar Flick keeps insisting he can become. Central to the attack, Robert Lewandowski continues to accept a reduced, more rotational role with professionalism, but on a day like this his penalty-box craft and veteran calm are invaluable. To his left, Marcus Rashford offers the north–south threat that stretches back lines and opens channels for Yamal and Olmo to exploit between the lines.

All of that attacking talent rests on the shoulders of young goalkeeper Joan Garcia, whose emergence has quietly solved what once looked like a looming crisis between the posts. With veterans like Wojciech Szczesny now clearly backing him up, Garcia’s ability to play out under pressure allows Flick to keep the back line high and the structure compact, a necessity against a Mallorca side that lives on transitions and set pieces.

Because for all the talk of Barça stretching their lead at the top, Mallorca are not the soft mid-table visitors their league position suggests. Jagoba Arrasate’s side arrive just two points above the relegation zone, but their recent form – two wins in three, including victories over Athletic Club and Sevilla – hints at a team finally finding its level. At the heart of their threat is battering-ram striker Vedat Muriqi, in the form of his life with nine goals in his last nine outings. Alongside him, Jan Virgili returns to Camp Nou as a genuine danger on the break after leaving Barça last summer.

The first meeting between these sides, on the opening weekend of the season, was chaotic: two early red cards for Mallorca, a controversial goal scored while a defender was down injured, and lingering criticism that Barça should have made more of a three-goal advantage against nine men. There will be no such margin for error this time. Mallorca are healthier at the back, more coherent in midfield, and emotionally motivated to prove that August’s collapse was an anomaly rather than a talent gap.

Flick knows this, which is why his midfield choices matter so much. Leaning on Casado’s legs today is a bet that Barça can control the chaos before it starts, winning second balls and closing the channels Muriqi loves to attack. It also allows De Jong to be deployed surgically, introduced when the game needs his ability to slow pulses and dictate rhythm rather than asking him to grind through ninety minutes at something less than full fitness.

The wider context of the day only heightens the stakes. A win would move Barça four points clear of Real Madrid, at least temporarily, before Los Blancos visit Mestalla for what is almost always a difficult night against Valencia. With a Copa del Rey semi-final first leg away to Atlético Madrid looming on the horizon, keeping the league pressure applied and the mood positive is essential.

Off the pitch, the club’s news cycle matches the intensity of the fixture list. In Friday’s news roundup, Barça learned that Atlético will indeed be their opponents in the Copa semis – a tie now reshaped by the injury ruling out Atleti midfielder Pablo Barrios from the first leg. On Barça’s side, there was more positive continuity: news that Fermín López has signed a contract extension through 2031, doubling down on his status as one of the faces of this new era.

Fermín hasn’t been shy about his ambitions either. The 22-year-old openly admitted that wearing the captain’s armband one day would be “a dream,” words that land differently when backed by the intensity he shows every time he steps onto the pitch. With club president Joan Laporta watching on and Flick trusting him with major minutes, Fermín increasingly looks like the emotional barometer of this squad – part goalscoring midfielder, part cultural bridge between the dressing room and the stands.

The women’s side added its own chapter to the feel-good atmosphere, dismantling Real Madrid 4–0 away in the Copa de la Reina behind a brace from Ewa Pajor and goals from Alexia Putellas and Salma Paralluelo. Barça Femení once again turned a Clásico into a showcase, reminding everyone that the club’s most dominant team continues to reside in the women’s dressing room.

Yet even there, reality intruded. The confirmation of a torn ACL for defender Laia Aleixandri is a brutal blow, both personally and tactically. Her intelligence and composure in the back line have become central to how Barça build, press and suffocate opponents. The coming months will test the squad’s depth and tactical flexibility as much as her own resilience in recovery.

So this Mallorca match drops into a broader narrative: a club that feels like it is accelerating again, but knows how quickly momentum can be lost. Flick’s Barça have been thrilling in recent weeks, piling up chances and rediscovering the swagger that felt missing not so long ago. The next step is turning that energy into cold, routine competence – three points at home against a dangerous but beatable opponent, with minimal drama and maximum control.

If that happens, Barça will end the night exactly where they want to be: clear at the top of the table, riding a six-game winning streak, and able to turn fully toward an Atlético semi-final that suddenly looks a touch more manageable. All while young leaders like Yamal, Fermín and Casado continue to grow into roles that, not long ago, would have felt impossibly big.

The season’s decisive stretch is coming fast. Today is about making sure Barça arrive there on their own terms.
