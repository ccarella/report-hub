Barcelona vs Atlético Madrid: Copa del Rey semi-final first leg preview

February 7, 2026
  • Kickoff: Thursday, February 12 at 8:00 AM CT (3:00 PM local) at the Riyadh Air Metropolitano in Madrid.
  • Where to watch (US): Expected live on ESPN+ (Copa del Rey rights holder); confirm in the ESPN app closer to kickoff.
  • Form guide: Barcelona come in scorching hot with 10 wins in their last 11 across all competitions; Atlético have steadied after a wobbly autumn and are unbeaten in LaLiga since mid-December.
  • Key absences: Both managers are juggling the usual winter knocks and rotation, but no major long-term new injuries have been reported for either side as of February 7.
  • Biggest storyline: Flick’s free-flowing, high-press Barça gets its sternest cup examination yet against Simeone’s ruthlessly efficient Atlético in a tie that could define both teams’ seasons.
  • Context: Barça sit top of LaLiga with Atleti third; this semi-final is a chance to maintain momentum and move one step from a domestic double.

Barcelona’s season under Hansi Flick has quietly shifted from "promising" to "genuinely formidable." Sitting top of LaLiga and marching through Europe, Barça now turn their attention to the Copa del Rey semi-final first leg away to Atlético Madrid at the Riyadh Air Metropolitano. It is the kind of tie that traditionally exposes flaws, tests depth, and tells you how real a title charge truly is.

The context could not be more enticing. In LaLiga, Barcelona lead the table, with Real Madrid and Atlético giving chase; Atleti currently sit third and have rediscovered much of their trademark defensive stability after a choppy autumn. Flick has injected a more vertical, aggressive interpretation of positional play, and Barcelona arrive in Madrid on a superb run: 10 wins in their last 11 matches in all competitions, including convincing league victories and statement results in the Champions League. Atlético, meanwhile, have quietly pieced together a strong domestic run of their own, highlighted by a 5–0 demolition of Real Betis in the Copa just days ago.

Recent history between these two sides has been unusually open. The last five meetings across league and cup have produced goals and drama: Barcelona hold the edge with three wins, one draw and one defeat in that span, including a 3–1 LaLiga win at home in December. Yet Simeone’s men have also shown they can hurt Barça in transition and on set pieces, and the atmosphere at the Metropolitano reliably drags Atlético several levels above their baseline.

Tactically, the matchup hinges on how well Barcelona handle Atlético’s press and direct threat once possession is turned over. Flick’s Barça build with a high defensive line and aggressive full-backs, inviting pressure to then exploit space between the lines. Atlético will gladly accept that invitation. Expect Atleti to alternate between a compact mid-block 4-4-2 and a more assertive pressing shape, trying to trap Barcelona’s pivots near the touchline and spring quick counters through their wide forwards. If Barça’s rest defense—particularly the positioning of the holding midfielder and the two centre-backs—is even slightly off, Atlético have the tools to punish them.

For Barcelona, the key attacking question is whether they can move the ball quickly enough to pull Atlético’s back line out of its comfort zone. Flick will look for sharp third-man combinations, quick diagonal switches and underlapping runs from midfield to destabilize Simeone’s usually well-drilled block. The wingers’ decision-making in the final third—when to attack their man 1v1 versus when to recycle—will be crucial in avoiding cheap turnovers in exposed zones. Set pieces may also be decisive: Barça have improved on attacking dead balls this season, while Atlético remain one of Europe’s most dangerous sides from corners and wide free kicks.

Players to watch on the Barcelona side include their creative hub between the lines, whose ability to receive under pressure and slip runners in behind has been central to Flick’s attacking blueprint, and the centre-forward tasked with pinning Atlético’s centre-backs and finishing the flurry of cut-backs and low crosses this system is designed to generate. At the back, the ball-playing centre-back pairing will have to balance bravery in possession with discipline against Atlético’s direct counters.

For Atlético, the spotlight falls on their leading forward, whose hold-up play and channel runs are essential to turning defensive actions into real chances, and on the industrious central midfield duo that must both disrupt Barcelona’s rhythm and provide the first pass in transition. Out wide, Atleti’s wing-backs or wide midfielders will be asked to cover enormous ground: squeezing in to deny central overloads, then exploding forward when space opens behind Barça’s advancing full-backs.

In terms of squad news, both sides enter the tie managing the usual winter fatigue and minor knocks, but there have been no widely reported new long-term injuries or suspensions significantly altering either starting XI as of February 7. That stability should allow Flick and Simeone to lean heavily on their preferred core players, with rotation more likely to arrive in the second leg or in the league fixtures that bookend this semi-final.

What is at stake goes beyond a place in the Copa del Rey final. For Barcelona, progressing would reinforce the narrative that Flick’s project is ahead of schedule, adding another front on which they are genuine contenders and further energizing a squad already brimming with confidence. For Atlético, eliminating the league leaders would reassert Simeone’s side as a force capable of derailing anyone’s season, while providing a tangible shot at silverware in a campaign where the margins at the top of LaLiga are razor-thin.

Expect a fiercely contested first leg, with Atlético trying to impose their intensity early and Barcelona trusting their structure to gradually take control of possession. Over two games, Barça’s attacking quality and depth might just tilt the balance, but the Metropolitano rarely lets visitors leave unscathed. Prediction for the first leg: a cagey, high-level battle that ends Atlético Madrid 1–1 Barcelona, setting up a knife-edge return at Montjuïc.
